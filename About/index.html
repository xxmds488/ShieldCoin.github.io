    Standalone wallets VS exchange wallets

    If you go to an exchange like stocks.exchange or Tradesatoshi you can see that there is an easy option to keep your different coins stored there and maybe you don't understand why you would use anything else. Well there are some differences between the safety and accessibility between these options. First of all a wallet on an exchange isn't all that bad, there are some positives that for some people outshine the negatives.
    One of the reasons why you would use a wallet on an exchange would be to easily keep track of all you coins. If you store your SHIELD on stocks.exchange, well you can easily sell them for BTC and at the same time keep track of your NXT. Having all your coins on an exchange also makes it that you can see all your coins and price changes on one page, which of course is a big plus. And when on a low or a high you can quickly sell or buy more SHIELD.
    There are also some things that are less great about an exchange wallet. An exchange could get hacked and exchanges don't have to refund your stolen funds. Of course your computer with the standalone wallet could also get hacked. But if you don't do anything wrong you'll be completely safe.

    Electrum wallets

    Besides the basic wallets SHIELD also has an Electrum wallet. Electrum wallets do the same as other wallets, they store your coins, in this case your SHIELD. Electrum wallets are a little different from the standerd QT wallets as they focus on speed and low resource usage. Electrum wallets are based on SPV or Simple Payment Verififacation. SVP verifies transactions without needing to download the entire SHIELD blockchain so its transaction confirmation times can drop by over 50%. So if you want to quickly move SHIELD from and to your standalone wallet an Electrum wallet would be a great choice. If you want to know more about Electrum click Here.

    QT wallets

    One of the wallets you see when you want to download a SHIELD wallet is the QT wallet. As we've already said, at this moment the SHIELD team would recommend using the QT wallet as Electrum is having some problems. So for the people that want to store their SHIELD in a QT wallet, or for those who just want to learn more, we'll explain what the QT wallet for SHIELD does. The QT wallets are wallets where you have to download the entire SHIELD blockchain so you can confirm transactions. If you want to use a QT wallet you have to download the updated blockchain everytime you want to make a transaction. If you haven't updated the wallet in a while it could take some time to download the entire blockchain and we if you want to use a QT wallet we recommend updating once a week so when you do need to make a transaction you don't have to wait. If you want to quickly pay with your SHIELD and don't want to wait untill it updates you shouldn't use a QT wallet. On PC's with limited storage downloading a coin's blockchain could possibly be hard. Bitcoin has a blockchain that is 157.53 GB. Although SHIELD won't have that large of a blockchain it could still be a concerning factor.

  • The SHIELD Roadmap

    Here we'll be trying to explain the SHIELD team's reasoning in picking the projects for the roadmap. You can find the SHIELD roadmap here.

    Can the roadmap still change?

    Everything project that is currently on the roadmap will be released. There could be some unexpected setbacks to the SHIELD team that could make us run little late, we won't be more than 2 weeks behind our roadmap at any given time. If we do get set back we'll make sure we get back on pace. Any changes will be made public immediately when we make the decision. The SHIELD team is working hard to improve the coin and will not give up.

    Why new wallets?

    Although the wallets we currently have aren't bad the Electrum wallets are in many ways better and easier to use. Storing SHIELD or any other coin for that matter is easier to do on an Electrum wallet. Although cryptocurrency is an anonymous alternative to normal fiat money, it isn't perfect yet. Using a tor/i2p wallet brings some upgrades to the anonymity of using cryptocurrency. Furthermore you ofcourse want to be able to acces your SHIELD on your phone.

    Why social-media intergration?

    Having to use a separate app to give someone money is a big disconvenience. That's why the SHIELD team is trying to make it possible to make payments on almost any social-media platform or chat system. You'll be able to send your mother some SHIELD for her birthday or even pay your friends back. All on one platform of your choosing.

    Why PoS?

    What is PoS?

    Proof of Stake or PoS basically means that if you have coins you get a percentage of those coins added to your wallet. The basic concepts of PoS and the few first implementations have a few problems, like whales that don't sell/buy anymore because they just get more coins from not doing anything with them. This ofcourse we'll be trying to solve.

    So why use it?

    If you know what PoW is you probably also know a few of the problems there are with the system. Fist of all it costs a lot of power to maintain to coin, which costs a lot of money. Having proof of stakes takes away some of the power that big mining facility's have over the coin. It also makes 51% attacks on the coin harder to maintain and often too expensive to try. With PoS SHIELD won't be controlled by the rich.

    Why Quantum proof?

    In the near future Quantum computers will be getting better and better and they will become more available to public. Even if it could be still some years untill the public would be able to use quantum computers it's still important to be quantum proof as nobody wants to lose all their money from day to day. The SHIELD team is fighting this with a quantum proof update released in Q4 of 2018. It will be released with Project SHIELD that will have some upgrades to the coin aswell as make it quantum proof.

  • SHIELD coin info

    Here we will be going a little deeper into the coin specs of SHIELD, if you're just looking for a list of all the SHIELD specs you can look Here.

    SHIELD Coin Specs

    SHIELD will have a total of 660 million coins, this makes paying with small amount of money at a time a little easier but still makes each coin worth enough.

    SHIELD has a blocktime of 15 seconds, this makes it extremely fast and easy to make payment almost eliminating the confirmation time.

    The SHIELD RPC port is: 20103. RPC is the port generally used by applications to interact with the SHIELD network and operate your SHIELD node/wallet

    The shield P2P port is: 21103. P2P is the port used by nodes to connect to the network and allow new nodes to connect to the network

    Mining SHIELD

    You can mine SHIELD with multiple algorithms which makes mining easier and better on different platforms. Mining SHIELD currencly is done with PoW and will be for a while but the SHIELD team is working on our PoS, PoS Boo. This update will be released with the Project Boo update that you can expect in Q3 of 2018. Our PoS will make the cost of maintaning the SHIELD blockchain lower and will keep electricity costs lower. PoS will also make the inflation of the coin lower and will give it a better future.
